Facts about Cape Reinga

Geographically speaking, New Zealand's North Island is home to the Aupouri Peninsula, which has Cape Reinga at its northwesterly most point. The distance to Kaitaia, the closest town, is about 100 kilometres. Reinga is a Maori-derived name that means "Underworld." According to the Maori people, it is a location where the spirits of the dead congregate before entering the underworld. It is also well-known for being the lone location where Te Rauparaha and his entire tribe arrived in Aotearoa for the first time. The region is well-known for being a sacred site for the Maori people.

Cape Reinga is a meeting point of seas

The stunning Cape Reinga is regarded as the boundary between the Tasman Sea, which approaches from the west, and the Pacific Ocean, which comes from the east. You can almost certainly see where the two bodies of water converge from the spectacular lighthouse. Unsteady waters develop off the shore as a result of the collision of the two water bodies. The stunning waves that are formed by the collision of the two bodies are amazing to observe from a distance.

The Spirits Travel to Cape Reinga on their Journey to the Afterlife

The Maori believe that Cape Reinga is a location where the spirits of the deceased pass by on their route to the afterlife. The Maori people also hold the view that the souls ascend the 800-year-old pohutukawa tree's roots on their way to the afterlife. The ghosts travel to the Three Kings Island for their final glimpse of the earth before leaving the mainland at Cape Reinga.
